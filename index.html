The HLM is a messenger based on the core of an anonymous network with theoretically provable anonymity of HLS. A feature of this messenger is the provision of anonymity of the fact of transactions (sending, receiving). The HLM can use HLT to download messages when the user was offline.

The HLS is the core of an anonymous network with theoretically provable anonymity, built on the basis of friend-to-friend (F2F) connections. It is based on the "queues" model. In certain implementations, it may exhibit the property of "abstractness".

The HLT is an application that emulates itself as a HLS for receiving messages from the network. It stores downloaded messages in its database for subsequent issuance. The database works as a ring structure in which old messages are overwritten with new ones.

The theory of the structure of hidden systems is a scientific and theoretical article describing the development of anonymous communications and secure applications. The analysis is based on a dialectical approach that allows us to consider the patterns of development through the sum of accumulated contradictions.
